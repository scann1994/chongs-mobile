Quality: About This Data Set

Is the data complete?

No. Out of 129 countries, 112 countries are missing entries for contraceptive prevalence, antenatal care coverage, births attended by skilled health personnel. My hypothesis is that those data was too hard to retrieve for local officials (due to sparse geography or large population), hence WHO couldn’t collect meaningful data for those features. I briefly searched online for data such as contraceptive use in China in 2012. There is data available, but it’s either limited to categories such as uses among married women or the methodology of data collection is unclear. My conclusion is that it is very difficult to recover the null values and that we will need to focus our exploration on under-5 mortality ratio and maternal mortality ratio in 1990, 2000 and 2010, i.e. the columns where a meaningful amount of data is available.

Is the data coherent?

Under-5 mortality rate column has a number of values over 100, which doesn’t make sense intuitively. I searched for the original WHO report and found out that the mortality rate refers to the number of deaths by age 5 per 1000 live births. Similarly, maternal mortality ratio refers to the number of deaths per 100,000 live births. My intuition is that the distribution of the mortality rates will follow a Poisson distribution. That is confirmed by the histograms displayed in exploratory analysis. We can also retrieve reasonable findings that the overall mortality rates decreased when we moved to 2000 and 2010. Similar trends can be found for maternal mortality ratios as well.

Is the data correct?

The estimates of mortality presented here have been derived wherever possible from death-registration data reported annually to WHO. For countries where such data are not available or are of poor quality, household surveys and censuses are used to prepare estimates of mortality rates and life expectancy. The source of data is credible as WHO has been publishing such data for decades and has been cited over its data for studies at renowned academic institutions.
