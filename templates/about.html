Data Quality

Data Completeness

My data is incomplete in a number of ways. First, my battery data is missing voltage and other battery informatin except for battery level as seen below. This may be due to my phone's battery sensors not working properly with AWARE or AWARE's own sensors problem.

In addition, my screen data is missing data points for hours between 7 and 12 as seen below. This seems like a systematic incomplteness, but I need to compare with other people's screen data to see if this is a general problem.

Due to the nature of such data, I cannot re-collect my own battery and screen data back in Feburary. I had to continue data analysis with such incomplete data, and the incomepleteness in screen data made a significant impact on my analysis.

Data Coherency

As noted in analysis part, there is a lack of coherency between the battery level charged and the time spent charging. I only charged for about 2 hours on Monday and Thursday, on which my battery level was charged for over 70%. It raises questions regarding whether AWARE's plugins were working properly in collect my sensors' data, since it may indicate that I was able to charge 70% battery within 2 hours while I needed 8 hours on other days to charge for an equivalent amount. Besides this issue, my screen data has some incoherency as well. The on/off modes are fairly evenly distirbuted while locked/unlcoked modes are fairly even as well. However, when I compare how many data points there were for on/off versus locked/unlocked for the week of 2/6/2017 to 2/12/2017, there are more data points for on/off than for locked/unlocked. This is counterintuitive because on/off statuses were not supposed to be mutually exclusive with locked/unlocked statuses, which means we should see as many data points for locked/unlocked as for on/off.

Data Correctness

I first thought that my screen data is not complete due to the conflicting screen statuses at the same time of the same day. Code 0 means off, 1 means on, 2 mean locked, and 3 means unlocked. After exploration, I find out that code 0 (off) and 1 (on) are mutually exclusive, while code 2 (locked) and 2 (unlocked) are mutually exclusive. I then decided to analyze my screen status by splitting it to two groups: one where I study the difference between on and off, and the other where I study the difference between locked and unlocked.

Other incorrectness I spotted was some rare occurences of battery charges data where my battery level was significant lower after charging than when I started charging, which doesn't make sense because even if I was using my phone while charging, my battery would not descrease significantly. During data cleaning, I checked and got rid of these rare occurences.
